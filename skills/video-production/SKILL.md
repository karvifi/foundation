# SKILL: Video Production

## Purpose
Create videos that hold attention, communicate clearly, and drive action.
Video is the most powerful content format — and the most wasted when done without structure.

## Pre-Production

### Step 1: Strategy Foundation
Before any scripting:
```
Goal: [what single action should viewer take after watching?]
Audience: [who exactly is this for? what do they already know?]
Length: [under 2 min for awareness, 5-15 min for education, 20+ for deep dive]
Platform: [YouTube / LinkedIn / TikTok / Loom / website] — affects style dramatically
Distribution: [how will this reach people?]
Success metric: [view count / watch time % / click-through / leads generated]
```

### Step 2: Story Structure

**The 5-Act Structure (for most videos)**:
```
Act 1 — HOOK (0-15 seconds, up to 30 for long videos)
  Purpose: Stop the scroll. Create a reason to keep watching.
  Formats:
  - Bold claim: "Most [category] are doing [common thing] wrong."
  - Problem statement: "If you've ever struggled with [pain], this video is for you."
  - Result first: "I went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]. Here's exactly how."
  - Question: "[Specific question your audience is asking]"
  - Visual hook: Show the end result before explaining how

Act 2 — PROMISE (15-45 seconds)
  Purpose: Tell them what they'll get by watching to the end.
  "In this video, I'm going to show you [specific outcome]."
  "By the end, you'll know [things they'll be able to do]."

Act 3 — CONTENT (the bulk)
  Purpose: Deliver the value promised.
  Structure: [Point 1] → [Point 2] → [Point 3]
  Each point: Teach → Example → Application
  Transitions: "Now that you know X, let's look at Y..."

Act 4 — SUMMARY
  Purpose: Reinforce the key takeaways.
  "Here's what we covered:"
  [3 bullets max — most important points]

Act 5 — CTA (last 30-60 seconds)
  Purpose: Tell them what to do next.
  One action only: subscribe / click link / download / comment
  Give them a reason: "If you want to go deeper on [topic], watch [video]"
```

**The Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS) for short videos**:
```
Problem (5-10 seconds): "Most people [do X wrong]..."
Agitate (10-20 seconds): "And this leads to [specific bad outcome]..."
Solve (30-90 seconds): "Here's what works instead: [solution]"
CTA (5 seconds): "Save this for next time you need it."
```

### Step 3: Script Writing

**Script format**:
```markdown
[TITLE]
[Platform | Length | Hook type]

---
HOOK [0:00 - 0:15]
VISUAL: [what appears on screen]
AUDIO: "[exact words spoken]"
TEXT OVERLAY: "[any text shown on screen]"

---
PROMISE [0:15 - 0:30]
VISUAL: [presenter on camera / b-roll / screen recording]
AUDIO: "By the end of this video, you'll know..."

---
SECTION 1: [Section title]
VISUAL: [description of visual]
AUDIO: "[script]"
B-ROLL: [description of b-roll to cut to]
CAPTION: "[key quote or stat to display on screen]"
```

**Writing for speech (not reading)**:
```
Rules:
  - Short sentences. Vary length. Never write how you'd write an article.
  - Contractions always: "you're" not "you are"
  - Conversational: "here's the thing" "now here's where it gets interesting"
  - Read aloud before recording — if you stumble, rewrite it
  - Avoid parenthetical asides — they don't work in speech
  - Land each point before moving on ("so that's [point 1]. Now let's talk about...")
```

### Step 4: Shot List

```markdown
# Shot List: [Video Title]

[ ] Medium shot (waist up) — primary talking head
[ ] Close up (face) — emphasis moments

## SECTION 1 SHOTS

[ ] B-roll 2: [description] — needed for [timestamp]
[ ] Screen recording: [what to capture]
[ ] Insert shot: [object/detail to show close]

## SECTION 2 SHOTS
[continue pattern]

## CUTAWAYS
[ ] Product/demo shot
[ ] Over-the-shoulder working shot
[ ] Results/outcome shot
```

## Production

### Recording Best Practices

**Audio (most important, often most neglected)**:
```
Microphone priority:
  1. Lavalier (lapel) mic — most consistent, works anywhere
  2. Shotgun mic on boom — best quality, needs positioning
  3. USB condenser mic — desk recording, excellent quality
  4. Built-in camera mic — avoid for anything professional

Recording environment:
  - Eliminate background noise first (HVAC, outside traffic)
  - Soft surfaces reduce echo (curtains, carpet, foam panels)
  - Record a 10-second silent room tone — used for audio cleanup
  - Clap once before each take — syncs audio and video
  
Monitoring: wear headphones while recording — you'll catch problems immediately
```

**Lighting**:
```
3-Point Lighting Setup:
  Key light: 45° to your left/right, slightly above eye level — main illumination
  Fill light: opposite side, dimmer — fills shadows (50% of key light brightness)
  Back light: behind you, pointed at back of head — separates you from background

Window light (free and often better):
  - Soft, diffused window light = professional look
  - Position: sit facing or at 45° to window
  - Avoid window behind you (creates silhouette)
  - Overcast days = ideal diffusion

Quick rule: Face should be evenly lit, background slightly dimmer
```

**Framing**:
```
Talking head: eyes at top third of frame (rule of thirds)
Leave looking space: face should point toward center of frame, not edge
Eye level: camera at eye height or very slightly above
Background: something of interest but not distracting (bookshelf, simple wall, plants)
```

## Post-Production

### Editing Workflow
```
Step 1: ASSEMBLY CUT
  - Lay down all clips in order
  - Keep everything, cut nothing
  - Just get the story in sequence

Step 2: ROUGH CUT
  - Cut obvious mistakes (long pauses, flubs, restarts)
  - Keep best takes of each section
  - Don't refine — just get the right material

Step 3: FINE CUT
  - Tighten pacing (remove every "um," "uh," "like" — or keep some for authenticity)
  - Add b-roll to cover cuts and illustrate points
  - Sync b-roll to words being spoken

Step 4: PICTURE LOCK
  - Final review of all cuts
  - No more moving clips after this point
  - Export proxy for color/audio work

Step 5: AUDIO MIX
  - Level dialogue: -12 to -6 dB (conversational, peaks at -6 dB)
  - Remove background noise (Adobe Audition, iZotope RX, built-in Premiere tools)
  - Add music bed: -20 to -30 dB under dialogue
  - Sound effects: match level to context (subtle)

Step 6: COLOR GRADE
  - Correct first: fix white balance, exposure issues
  - Then grade: apply consistent look (LUT or manual)
  - Keep it clean — the worst color grade is obvious color grading

Step 7: GRAPHICS
  - Lower thirds (name, title)
  - Text overlays for key stats or quotes
  - Chapter titles for longer videos
  - Thumbnail frame identification

Step 8: FINAL EXPORT
  - YouTube: H.264, 1080p minimum (4K preferred), CBR 10-20 Mbps
  - Instagram: H.264, 1080×1920 (vertical) or 1080×1080 (square)
  - LinkedIn: H.264, 1920×1080
  - Web: H.264 or H.265 (smaller file), 1080p, optimize file size
```

### Thumbnail Creation
```
Elements of a high-CTR thumbnail:
  1. Face with strong emotion (curiosity, surprise, determination)
  2. Text: 3-5 words max, readable at small size
  3. Contrast: subject stands out from background
  4. Visual tension or curiosity gap
  5. Color consistency with your channel brand

Tools: Canva, Adobe Express, Photoshop
Size: 1280×720 (16:9 ratio)
File size: under 2MB

A/B test thumbnails: TubeBuddy or VidIQ for YouTube
```

## Output
- Complete script with visual descriptions
- Shot list
- Production checklist
- Post-production workflow document
- Distribution plan (platform-specific formats)

## Quality checks
- [ ] Script read aloud before recording — no stumbles
- [ ] Hook is in first 15 seconds and creates clear reason to watch
- [ ] One clear CTA (not multiple)
- [ ] Audio recorded clean — no background noise
- [ ] Every "um" and "uh" removed (or intentionally kept)
- [ ] Thumbnail tested (looks good at thumbnail size, not full size)
