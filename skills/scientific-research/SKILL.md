# SKILL: Scientific Research

## Purpose
Conduct rigorous, reproducible, credible research on any topic.

## Process

### Step 1: Research Question Formulation
Use PICO or PICOT framework:
- **P** — Population/Problem: Who or what is being studied?
- **I** — Intervention/Phenomenon: What is the intervention or topic?
- **C** — Comparison: What is being compared to?
- **O** — Outcome: What are you measuring?
- **T** — Time: Over what period?

### Step 2: Literature Review Protocol
Search strategy:
- PubMed, Semantic Scholar, Google Scholar, arXiv, SSRN
- Search terms: primary terms + synonyms + MeSH terms
- Date filter: last 5-10 years (unless historical research)
- Screening: title/abstract → full text → inclusion/exclusion criteria

Synthesis:
- Evidence map: what is known / what is contested / what is unknown
- Quality assessment: study design hierarchy
  (Meta-analysis > RCT > Cohort > Case-control > Expert opinion)
- Identify gaps in literature → your contribution

### Step 3: Methodology Selection
| Research Question Type | Method |
|----------------------|--------|
| Causal relationship | RCT or Quasi-experimental |
| Correlation/association | Cross-sectional or Cohort |
| Lived experience | Qualitative (interviews, ethnography) |
| Best of both | Mixed methods |
| Existing data | Systematic review or Meta-analysis |
| Exploratory | Grounded theory or Case study |

### Step 4: Data Collection Plan
- Sample size calculation (power analysis)
- Sampling strategy (random, stratified, purposive)
- Data collection instruments (validated scales preferred)
- Ethics approval pathway
- Data management plan (storage, anonymization, retention)

### Step 5: Analysis Plan (pre-registered if possible)
Quantitative:
- Descriptive statistics first
- Inferential tests matching data type and distribution
- Effect sizes + confidence intervals (not just p-values)
- Multiple comparison correction if needed

Qualitative:
- Thematic analysis or Framework analysis
- Member checking for validity
- Audit trail documentation

### Step 6: Reporting Standards
- CONSORT (RCTs)
- STROBE (observational studies)
- PRISMA (systematic reviews)
- COREQ (qualitative)
- Report null results (publication bias awareness)

## Output
- Research question (PICO format)
- Literature review summary
- Methodology justification
- Analysis plan
- Results framework
- Limitations section

## Quality checks
- [ ] Research question is answerable and specific
- [ ] Methodology matches research question
- [ ] Sample size justified
- [ ] Bias sources identified and addressed
- [ ] Analysis plan pre-specified (not post-hoc)
