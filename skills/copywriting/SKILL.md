# SKILL: Copywriting

## Purpose
Write words that make people take action. Not just clear — persuasive and converting.

## Core Formulas

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solve)
Best for: emails, short ads, intro paragraphs
```
Problem: State the exact pain the reader has
Agitate: Make them feel the cost of NOT solving it
Solve: Present your solution as the answer
```

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
Best for: long-form pages, email sequences
```
Attention: Hook that stops the scroll
Interest: Relevant information that builds curiosity
Desire: Create want through benefits and proof
Action: Clear, low-friction CTA
```

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
Best for: testimonials, case studies, social ads
```
Before: What life looks like now (the pain)
After: What life looks like with your solution
Bridge: Your product is how they get from Before to After
```

## Headline Formulas (test 3-5 variants)
```
How to [achieve outcome] without [common pain]
[Number] [adjective] ways to [achieve outcome]
The [adjective] guide to [topic]
[Outcome] in [time period] — [mechanism]
Why [common belief] is wrong (and what to do instead)
If you can [simple action], you can [desired outcome]
[Name/type of person]: [specific benefit you might not know about]
```

## The 5 Objections to Handle
Every piece of copy must address:
1. I don't have time
2. I can't afford it
3. It won't work for me specifically
4. I don't believe you
5. I don't need it right now

## CTA Formulas
```
Bad CTA: "Submit" / "Click here" / "Learn more"
Good CTA: [Action verb] + [specific outcome]

Examples:
- "Get my free [resource]"
- "Start building in 5 minutes"
- "See how [customer] got [result]"
- "Claim your [discount/bonus] before [deadline]"
```

## Voice & Tone Guide
- Write at grade 8 reading level (Hemingway App test)
- Short sentences (average < 20 words)
- Active voice, not passive
- "You" not "our users" or "customers"
- Specific > vague ("increase conversions by 34%" not "improve results")
- Show, don't tell ("Trusted by 12,000 marketers" not "trusted by many")

## Quality checks
- [ ] Headline is benefit-focused (not clever, not vague)
- [ ] Every paragraph has a job (delete if it doesn't)
- [ ] Top 5 objections addressed
- [ ] CTA is specific (action verb + outcome)
- [ ] Hemingway score ≤ grade 10
- [ ] At least 3 social proof elements
